# Demo Script — showing the demo line to a prospect

*Total runtime: ~7 minutes live + close. Everything below uses YOUR phone on speaker, in front of them. Nothing is staged — that's the pitch.*

## Setup (before the meeting)
- [ ] `fake_now` is **null** (line in day/night mode matching real time — decide which story you want; night mode demos emergencies better)
- [ ] Your Google Calendar open on the laptop, today/tomorrow visible
- [ ] Your cell charged, on speaker, not on Do Not Disturb
- [ ] Know their trade — you can say "we configure this per business; for you it'd say plumbing, not HVAC"

## 1. Frame it (30 seconds)
> "Quick math before I show you anything. How many calls a week do you think ring out or hit voicemail? … Industry data says the majority of callers who hit voicemail don't leave a message — they call the next company on Google. If your average job is $[their number], that's what one missed call costs. I'm going to show you what happens to those calls instead. This is a live phone number — call it with me."

## 2. The call (2–3 min) — hand THEM your phone or dial on speaker
Let them hear the greeting — point at the phone when it says **"AI assistant"**:
> "Notice it says it's an AI up front. Customers don't get tricked, and it never gets caught lying."

Coach them to play a customer: *"tell it your AC died this morning."* Let it collect name, address, issue naturally. Prompt them to ask **"wait, is this a real person?"** — the graceful answer is a reliable laugh and the moment people relax.

## 3. The money shot (60 seconds)
When it offers appointment slots, tell them to take one. The instant the agent confirms:
> "Look at the laptop."
**The booking is already on the Google Calendar.** Pause. Let them stare.
> "That's a real appointment on a real calendar, booked by the phone assistant, while we talked. Your techs would just… see it and go."

## 4. The safety net (90 seconds)
> "Now the part nobody else shows you — what happens when someone *doesn't* talk to it."

Call the line again and **hang up after two rings.** Hold up your phone: the text-back arrives in seconds. Reply "my water heater is leaking" and let them watch the SMS agent respond, ask a smart question, offer times.
> "Hang-ups become text conversations. Text conversations become bookings. Nothing leaks."

## 5. Emergencies (60 seconds — night mode makes this land harder)
> "Last one. It's 2am and a customer has no heat. Watch what happens to MY phone — pretend I'm your on-call tech."

Call, describe an emergency, accept the connect offer → your cell rings live in front of them. Optionally reject it and show the 🚨 SMS + page call that follow.
> "Connected in one call. And if your tech doesn't pick up, it takes details, promises a callback, and blows up the tech's phone with the summary. Gas smell? It tells them to get out and call 911 — hard rule, every client, not configurable."

## 6. Show the receipts (60 seconds)
Open the owner texts you've received during the demo (after-hours summary, emergency alert), and if they're analytical, the monthly report:
> "You get a text digest at 6pm daily and this report monthly: calls answered, after-hours saves, emergencies, bookings, and a conservative estimate of revenue captured. You'll know exactly what it's earning."

## 7. Close
> "Setup on your side is one dial code on your business phone — takes two minutes and reverses with one dial code, here's the sheet [hand them the forwarding handout]. Your number never changes, your line never changes. We turn it on, and for two weeks every call it saves gets logged. Then the numbers argue for me. Want to pick a start date?"

## Objection cheat-sheet
- **"My customers hate robots."** — "They hate *voicemail*. You heard it — it's honest about being an AI and it solves their problem in two minutes. The alternative isn't a human; it's silence."
- **"What if it says something wrong?"** — "It can only quote prices you've approved, it never promises arrival times, and anything it can't handle becomes a text to your phone within seconds. You saw the emergency handoff."
- **"What's it cost to run?"** — "Pennies a minute; a single saved job typically covers a month. And the report shows the capture math so you're never guessing."
