# Never Miss Another Call — Setup Guide for Your Phone

*(Client-facing handout. Replace **[YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER]** with the number we provisioned for you.)*

Your business line stays exactly as it is. We simply tell your carrier: **"if we don't pick up, or the line is busy, send the call to our 24/7 assistant instead of voicemail."** Callers reach a friendly assistant that answers instantly, takes their info, books appointments, and gets emergencies to your on-call tech.

**And it's 100% reversible in one dial code** — you're never locked in.

## Turn it ON (conditional forwarding — rings your phone first, always)

Dial these from the business phone itself:

### AT&T (and most GSM carriers)
| What | Dial |
| --- | --- |
| Forward when unanswered | `*61*` [YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER] `#` then press call |
| Forward when busy | `*67*` [YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER] `#` then press call |
| Forward when unreachable (dead battery/no signal) | `*62*` [YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER] `#` then press call |

### Verizon
| What | Dial |
| --- | --- |
| Forward when unanswered / busy | `*71` [YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER] then press call |

### T-Mobile
| What | Dial |
| --- | --- |
| Forward when unanswered | `**61*` [YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER] `#` then press call |
| Forward when busy | `**67*` [YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER] `#` then press call |
| Forward when unreachable | `**62*` [YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER] `#` then press call |

### Office VoIP systems (RingCentral, Grasshopper, Ooma, Nextiva, etc.)
In your admin portal, look for **Call Handling / Call Forwarding → "When unanswered"** (sometimes "after X rings" or "no answer destination"). Set the destination to [YOUR CAPTURE NUMBER] instead of voicemail. Most systems also have a "when busy" rule — set that too. Two minutes, no hardware. We're happy to hop on a screen-share and do it with you.

### Landlines (copper/fiber from the phone company)
Usually `*92` [number] for no-answer forwarding and `*90` [number] for busy (varies by provider — we'll confirm with yours).

## Turn it OFF (any time, instantly)
| Carrier | Dial |
| --- | --- |
| AT&T | `#61#`, `#67#`, `#62#` (or `##002#` to clear all forwarding) |
| Verizon | `*73` |
| T-Mobile | `##61#`, `##67#`, `##62#` (or `##002#` to clear all) |
| VoIP | Flip the same setting back to voicemail |

That's the whole exit process. Your number, your control.

## Quick test after setup
Call your business line from another phone and just don't answer. After a few rings you should hear: *"Thanks for calling [Your Business]! This is the AI assistant…"* — then hang up and check that you got a text summary.

## A note on call recording *(only applies if recording is enabled for your account)*
Calls can be recorded so you get accurate summaries and a record of what customers asked for. Recording laws vary by state: most states require only one party's consent, but **some states — including California, Florida, Illinois, Maryland, Massachusetts, Michigan, Montana, Nevada, New Hampshire, Pennsylvania, and Washington — require all parties to consent.** If you operate in or take calls from those states, we recommend a disclosure line in the greeting ("this call may be recorded"), which we can enable for you, or we can turn recording off entirely. This is general information, not legal advice — check with your attorney for your specific situation.
